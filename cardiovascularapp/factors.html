Cardiovascular Disease

Factors of Cardiovascular Disease

Framingham Heart Study

  1. A landmark research project that began in 1948 which followed a large group of people who developed Cardiovascular heart diseases over time.

  2. Its main purpose was to identify common factors in patients in order to be able to calculate the "risk factors"

  3. The researchers recruited 11,000+ men & women to in a certain age range to examine.

Data Set

I studied several different factors from the data including:

  1. Diagnosis (yes/no)

  2. Cholesterol

  3. Smoking Status

  4. Age

  5. Blood Pressure

  6. Diabetic Status

  7. Sex

  8. BMI

  9. Glucose Level

  10. Family History

  11. Race

  12. Obesity

  13. Tobacco Intake

My main goal was to learn how the different factors impacted whether or not the patient was diagnosed with a cardiovascular disease.

Significant Factors

  1. Sex: A male patient is 16.2 percent more likely to have CHD

  2. Diabetic Status: A patient with diabetes is 15.5 percent more likely to have CHD than a patient without

  3. Systolic Blood Pressure: As blood pressure increases by 1, risk increases by 0.2 percent

  4. Cholesterol: As cholesterol increases by 1, their risk increases by 0.1 percent

  5. Age: As age increases by 1, the risk increases by 0.8 percent

  2. Cholesterol
    • There are two types of cholesterol in the body. One is called Low-Density Lipoprotein and another is called High-Density Lipoprotein
    • Foods that increase Low-Density Lipoprotein include the following: Burgers, French Fries, Pizza, Fried Chicken Sandwiches, Chips, Onion Rings, and processed meat like bacon
    • Foods that increase High-Density Lipoprotein includes the following: Avocados, Salmon, Tuna, Olives, Nuts and Seeds, Oats, Fruits and Vegatables, and evenDark chocolate in moderation
  3. Blood Pressure
    • Make time to exercise consistently
    • For example, if you have 15 minutes to spare, do some pushups or squats. You can even do simple stretches to get the blood flowing
    • It's also ideal follow a balanced diet like myPlate
  4. Diet
    • If the majority of your diet includes high amounts of saturated fat and cholesterol, then the risk of CVD or atheroscherosis increases tremendously
  5. Tobacco Intake
    • One of the strongest, if not, the strongest predictor of CVD
  6. Obesity
    • If your BMI level is greater than 30, then it's higher than normal
