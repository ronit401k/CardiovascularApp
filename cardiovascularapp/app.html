Information on the Heart

  1. The heart facilitates all bloodflow in the body
  2. It's just as important, if not more important than the brain
  3. However, the hearts of many stopped pumping blood as a result of cardiovascular disease

What is cardiovascular disease

  1. Diseases related to the heart such as heart attacks, strokes, valve problems and the most prevalent, Coronary Art Disease
  2. Cardiovascular disease is also the leading cause of deaths worldwide, particularly in America
  3. Symptoms
    • Chest pain or discomfort
    • Irregular Heartbeats aka arrhythmias
  4. Main Signs
    • Heart Attack
    • Stroke

Factors of Cardiovascular Disease

  1. Diabetic status
    • Diabetes
      • Type 2
        • Makes it twice as likely for Cardiovascular Disease
  2. Age
  3. Sex
  4. Cholesterol
  5. Blood Pressure

Types of Heart Disease

  1. Coronary Heart Disease
    • Atheroclerosis happens when plaque accumulates on the arterial walls. This leads to them becoming rigid and narrow, restricting the flow of blood
    • Angina Rectoris: Severe chest pains
    • Myocardial Infarction: Heart attack when one or more of the heart arteries are blocked
    • Arrhythmia: Non-standard heart rhythm which leads to cardiac arrest
  2. Heart Attack
    • Lower jaw pain
    • Middle back pain
    • Left arm pain
    • Shortness of Breath
  3. Stroke
    • Blockage that occurs on the way to the brain, rupture in the arteries of the brain which is also known as a brain attack
    • Types of Strokes
      • Ischemic
      • Hemorrhagic
      • Trans-ischemic or TIA
  4. Cincinnati Stroke Scale
    • Face: Check for smile and ask the question, Is one side like the other?
    • Arm: Check for gross body defects
    • Speech: Is their speech slurred
    • Time: If one of the previous three is the case, call 911

How to Prevent Heart Disease

  1. Monitor levels and intake of cholesterol, specifically cholesterol high in LDL (Low-Density Lipoprotein)
  2. Check Blood Pressure
  3. Your diet should be rich in food and vegetables. However, there should be a balance between Foods, Vegetables, Whole Grains, and HDL (High-Density Lipoprotien) fats
  4. Exercise frequently
  5. Maintain a healthy weight and low BMI
  6. Limit salt intake
  7. Limit use of tobacco
  8. Regulate diabetic levels
