Cardiovascular Disease

Heart Information

  1. The main function of the heart is to circulate blood to all your organs. The heart is a muscular organ, about the size of a fist, located in the center of the chest slightly to the left. It functions as a pump within the circulatory system, continuously circulating blood to deliver oxygen and nutrients to the body's tissues and remove waste products like carbon dioxide. It is divided into four chambers—the right and left atria and the right and left ventricles—that work together to pump deoxygenated blood to the lungs and oxygenated blood to the rest of the body.
  2. It's just as important, if not more important than the brain
  3. Unfortunately, many people suffer from heart disease, and Heart Disease is the leading cause of deaths.
